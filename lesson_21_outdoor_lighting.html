‚òÄÔ∏è Understanding Natural Sunlight

Before we start creating outdoor lighting, let's understand what makes natural sunlight unique and how to recreate its characteristics in Poser.

üåç How Sunlight Actually Works

Key Characteristics of Sunlight

  • Parallel rays: Sun is so far away, rays are effectively parallel (no falloff)
  • Single direction: All shadows point the same way
  • Very bright: Much brighter than artificial lights
  • Color changes: Varies from cool (noon) to warm (sunrise/sunset)
  • Sharp shadows: Creates defined shadows (on clear days)
  • Sky light fill: Blue sky acts as natural fill light

üîÜ Sun Angle Throughout the Day

Sun Position Guidelines

  • Sunrise/Sunset: 5-15¬∞ above horizon (very low angle)
  • Early Morning/Late Afternoon: 20-40¬∞ above horizon
  • Mid-Morning/Mid-Afternoon: 45-60¬∞ above horizon
  • Noon (summer): 60-80¬∞ above horizon (nearly overhead)

Rule of thumb: Lower sun = longer shadows and warmer colors. Higher sun = shorter shadows and cooler colors.

üïê Lighting by Time of Day

Let's learn to create lighting for different times of day. Each time has its own character and mood.

‚òÄÔ∏è Midday Sun (Noon)

Setup for Bright Daylight

Sun (Infinite Light):

  • Angle: 70-80¬∞ above horizon (nearly overhead)
  • Color: Cool white (255, 255, 250)
  • Intensity: 120-150%
  • Shadows: Hard, short

Sky Fill (Point Light or IBL):

  • Cool blue (180, 210, 255)
  • Intensity: 30-40% of sun
  • No shadows

Mood: Bright, neutral, sometimes harsh. Good for clear, well-lit scenes but can be unflattering for portraits.

üåÖ Golden Hour

Setup for Magic Hour Lighting

Sun (Infinite Light):

  • Angle: 10-20¬∞ above horizon (low angle)
  • Color: Golden orange (255, 200, 140)
  • Intensity: 100-130%
  • Shadows: Long, warm

Sky Fill (Point Light):

  • Warm pink/orange (255, 220, 200)
  • Intensity: 30-40% of sun
  • From opposite side of sun

Mood: Romantic, beautiful, warm. The most flattering natural light. Perfect for portraits and emotional scenes.

üåá Sunset

Setup for Dramatic Sunset

Sun (Infinite Light):

  • Angle: 0-10¬∞ above horizon (at horizon level)
  • Color: Deep orange-red (255, 140, 100)
  • Intensity: 80-100%
  • Shadows: Very long, dramatic

Sky Fill (Point Light):

  • Purple-blue (200, 180, 255)
  • Intensity: 20-30% of sun
  • Creates color contrast

Mood: Dramatic, intense, nostalgic. Creates strong color contrasts. Great for silhouettes.

üå§Ô∏è Overcast Day

Setup for Cloudy/Diffused Light

Option 1 - Soft Infinite Light:

  • No specific sun position (clouds diffuse it)
  • Color: Cool neutral (240, 245, 250)
  • Intensity: 80-100%
  • Shadows: Very soft or disabled
  • Shadow blur: 20-30 if enabled

Option 2 - IBL Only:

  • Use overcast sky HDRI
  • No additional lights needed
  • Creates perfectly diffused, natural light

Mood: Soft, even, neutral. Minimizes shadows. Good for preventing harsh lighting, revealing detail in shadows.

üåç Using IBL for Outdoor Scenes

IBL (Image-Based Lighting) is often the best choice for outdoor scenes because it captures the complete natural environment ‚Äì sun, sky, atmospheric effects, and all.

‚úÖ Advantages of IBL for Outdoors

  • Instant realism: Automatically captures natural light distribution
  • Complete environment: Sun + sky + ambient in one
  • Authentic colors: Real-world color temperature baked in
  • Natural reflections: Figures reflect actual environment
  • Time-saving: One light does everything

üéØ IBL Setup for Outdoor Scenes

Step-by-Step IBL Outdoor Setup

  1. Choose appropriate HDRI:
    • Sunny day: "outdoor_sunny" HDRIs
    • Golden hour: "sunset" or "golden_hour" HDRIs
    • Overcast: "overcast" or "cloudy" HDRIs
  2. Load in Material Room:
    • Material Room ‚Üí Background ‚Üí Background Color
    • Add Image Map node ‚Üí Load .hdr file
  3. Enable IBL rendering:
    • Render Settings ‚Üí Use Background Picture: ON
    • Render Settings ‚Üí Image Based Lighting: ON
    • IBL Quality: Medium for tests, High for finals
  4. Adjust intensity:
    • Sunny day: 1.2-1.5
    • Golden hour: 1.0-1.3
    • Overcast: 0.8-1.2
  5. Rotate for best angle:
    • Rotate HDRI to position sun optimally
    • Front-side lighting usually most flattering

‚òÄÔ∏è Traditional Sun Lighting Setup

Sometimes you want more control than IBL offers. Here's how to create outdoor lighting with traditional lights.

üîÜ The Two-Light Outdoor System

Light 1: The Sun (Infinite Light)

  • Type: Infinite Light
  • Position: Doesn't matter (only rotation)
  • Angle: Based on time of day (see earlier section)
  • Color: Based on time of day
  • Intensity: 120-150% for daytime
  • Shadows: ON, blur 5-8

Light 2: Sky Fill (Point Light)

  • Type: Point Light
  • Position: Above scene, opposite side from sun
  • Color: Cool blue (180, 210, 255) for daytime
  • Intensity: 30-40% of sun
  • Shadows: OFF

üå¶Ô∏è Weather and Atmospheric Effects

‚òÄÔ∏è Clear Sunny Day

  • Strong sun (Infinite 130-150%)
  • Weak cool fill (30-40%)
  • Hard shadows (blur 5-8)
  • High contrast

‚õÖ Partly Cloudy

  • Moderate sun (100-120%)
  • Stronger fill (40-50%)
  • Medium shadows (blur 10-12)
  • Balanced look

‚òÅÔ∏è Overcast/Cloudy

  • Soft sun or no sun (80-100%)
  • Strong fill (60-80%)
  • Very soft shadows (blur 20-30) or no shadows
  • Low contrast, even light

üîÄ Hybrid: Combining IBL and Lights

The most powerful approach: use IBL for realism, add lights for control.

üí° The Hybrid Setup

  1. Base: IBL (intensity 0.8-1.0)
    • Provides realistic environment lighting
    • Natural reflections and ambient
  2. Enhancement: Spotlight (intensity 0.5-1.0)
    • Adds extra emphasis on face
    • Gives you artistic control
    • Position as you would key light
  3. Result: Realistic base + artistic control
