Hand Anatomy Basics

Four Key Components

1. Palm

The stable foundation. Can cup or flatten but remains relatively rigid.

2. Thumb

Only 2 joints (not 3). Opposes other fingers. Essential for grasping.

3. Fingers

Each has 3 joints: base (knuckle), middle, and tip. Each finger has unique characteristics.

4. Wrist

Controls overall hand angle and positioning.

The Cascade Rule

When fingers curl naturally:

  • Base joint curls MOST (60¬∞)
  • Middle joint curls LESS (45¬∞)
  • Tip joint curls LEAST (25¬∞)
  • Result: Smooth natural arc, not zigzag!

The Natural Resting Hand

This is your baseline for ALL hand poses!

Natural Rest Formula

  • Base joint: 15-20¬∞ curl
  • Middle joint: 10-15¬∞ curl
  • Tip joint: 5-10¬∞ curl
  • Finger spacing: Slightly spread (not together)
  • Thumb: 45¬∞ angle from index finger
  • Variation: Each finger 2-5¬∞ different
üí° The 80/20 Rule: 80% of hand poses are variations on natural rest. Master this, and you're 80% there!

Seven Key Principles

1. The Cascade Rule

Base curls most ‚Üí middle less ‚Üí tip least. Creates natural arc.

2. Asymmetry Always

Every finger should be slightly different. Perfect symmetry looks robotic.

3. Thumb Opposition

Thumb swings ACROSS palm when grasping, not alongside fingers.

4. Finger Independence

Index is most independent. Ring and middle move together. Pinky is expressive.

5. Point and Curl Contrast

When one finger points, others naturally curl slightly.

6. Tension Reveals Emotion

Tight curls = stress/anger. Loose curls = relaxed. Open = confident.

7. Contact Shows Weight

Fingers flatten slightly when touching surfaces or objects.

Common Hand Gestures

Open Palm (Honesty, Stop)

  • Fingers mostly straight with subtle curve
  • Slightly spread
  • Palm facing viewer
  • Meaning: openness, honesty, yielding

Fist (Anger, Power)

  • Fingers curl tight (80-90¬∞ base)
  • Thumb wraps OVER fingers (not inside!)
  • Compact shape
  • Meaning: determination, anger, strength

Pointing (Direction, Emphasis)

  • Index straight
  • Other fingers curl progressively
  • Thumb relaxed or extended
  • Meaning: direction, accusation, emphasis

Peace Sign / Victory

  • Index and middle extended in V
  • Ring and pinky curled
  • Thumb holds down curled fingers
  • Meaning: peace, victory, casual pose

Relaxed Hang (Neutral)

  • Gentle curves in all fingers
  • No tension
  • Each finger slightly different
  • Meaning: neutral, at ease, relaxed

Top 10 Common Mistakes

1. Wooden Plank Hands

Problem: All fingers perfectly straight
Fix: Add natural rest curl to every finger

2. Identical Finger Curls

Problem: All fingers same angle
Fix: Vary each by 5-10 degrees

3. Equal Joint Bends

Problem: All joints bent equally (zigzag)
Fix: Follow cascade rule

4. Thumb Like a Finger

Problem: Thumb parallel to fingers
Fix: Angle away or swing across palm

5. Fingers Pressed Together

Problem: No spacing between fingers
Fix: Add 5-15¬∞ spread
